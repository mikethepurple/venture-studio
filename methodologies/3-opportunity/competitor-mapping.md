# Competitor Mapping

## When to use

Always runs in step 3. Maps the competitive landscape around this solution to find positioning gaps — where incumbents are weak, where the market is over-served, and where there's space for a new entrant.

## Inputs needed

- brief.md
- step-2/decisions.md

## Process

1. **Discover competitors:** Search G2, Capterra, ProductHunt, AlternativeTo, Reddit, and Crunchbase for solutions competing with this one. Include direct competitors (same value proposition, same customer), indirect (same problem, different approach), and adjacent (related problem). Target 15-25 companies.

2. **Profile top 8:** For each major competitor, find: positioning/tagline, pricing, target customer, key strengths (from reviews), key weaknesses (from reviews), funding stage.

3. **Build positioning map:** Choose two meaningful axes (e.g., price vs. scope, self-serve vs. full-service). Place competitors. Identify crowded quadrants and empty quadrants.

4. **Identify gaps:**
   - Who is underserved? (customer segment no one focuses on)
   - What job is underdone? (workflow step everyone ignores)
   - What's the pricing gap? (customers who can't afford existing options)
   - What's the trust gap? (reasons customers don't trust existing tools)
